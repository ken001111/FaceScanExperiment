# Scene 41069021 (ARKitScenes laser-GT) — 6-arm depth-supervision ablation

GeoSVR lidar-fork, 20k iters/arm, identical configs except depth supervision.
GT: Faro laser via highres_depth back-projection (frame-rendered -> GT coverage
== frame coverage by construction; observation-based culling is circular).
Protocol v6: eval region = GT within 3.0m of camera trajectory (96% of GT);
accuracy uses 100mm laser-support crop (GT lacks ceiling). ICP 20mm per arm.

## Conclusions

1. **Metric depth supervision works**: all three LiDAR-anchored arms roughly
   halve median completeness error and +60% F@10 vs photometric-only.
2. **Mono-alone is actively harmful**: worse than no depth on every metric
   (scale drift shrinks the scene; comp_mean 398 vs 308). Strongest
   motivation slide for metric anchoring.
3. **Fused ~ raw LiDAR here (within 2-5mm / 2pp)**: ARKitScenes rooms are a
   LiDAR-optimal regime (matte surfaces, 0.5-3m, dense returns). Fusion costs
   ~nothing where LiDAR is dense; its upside (LiDAR-degraded regions: close
   range, hair, specular skin) does not exist in this scene. The fused>lidar
   claim must be shown on face captures, not room scans.
4. Absolute numbers are modest (acc ~35mm, support ~0.5): GeoSVR at 20k iters
   on room-scale iPhone data has a fog floor (PSNR ~20.6); support_frac is
   the fog indicator and improves with depth supervision (0.35 -> 0.49).
5. **Seeded init recovers what fused-alone lost, back to raw-LiDAR level**: the
   full method (fused loss + LiDAR-seeded voxel init) restores accuracy 36.7->34.3
   and F@20 0.292->0.333 vs fused, landing on par with the raw-LiDAR arm
   (acc 34.2, F@20 0.336) and posting the best ICP fitness (0.191). Consistent
   with the LiDAR-optimal-regime story: in a dense-return room the seeded arm
   cannot beat raw LiDAR, only match it — the seeded/fused advantage still has to
   be demonstrated on face captures (close range / hair / specular skin).
